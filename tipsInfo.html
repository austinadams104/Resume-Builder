Unbreakable Rules

Did you know 17% of hiring managers only spend an average of 30 seconds looking at resumes? And 68% say they spend less than two minutes.

Hiring Managers will automatically dismiss a canidate from consideration for:

  • Resumes with typos - 61%
  • Resumes that copied large amounts of wording from the job posting - 41%
  • Resumes with an inappropriate email address - 35%
  • Resumes that don't include a list of skills - 30%

Five Mistakes to Avoid the Trash Bin

  • Mistake 1: Typos (grammatical, spelling, etc)
  • Mistake 2: Length (Typically only have 1 page for every 10 years of experience)
  • Mistake 3: Formatting (Distracting colors, too plain, hard to read)
  • Mistake 4: Confidential/Protected information (religion, race, gender)
  • Mistake 5: Lies

Resume Formats

  • Chronological/Traditional: Highlights work experience
    --particulatly work experience related to your field
  • Skills/Functional: Focuses on skills gained
    (through work experience, service, classes)
  • Hybrid/Combination: Combines related work experience
    and skills
    • Ideal for candidates with related work experience and skills enhanced majors
    • Newcomers to the job market can list education, volunteer work, and other
      experiences with an emphasis on skills gained from these experiences.

Resume Content

What goes on a Resume?

  • Education: post-secondary
  • Experience: related and other
  • Volunteer/service experience
  • Skills

  • Projects
  • Interests

  • Extra-curricular activities

Success Statements

A good success statement used in effective resumes should:

  • Start with an action verb and briefly descrive a skill, responsibility, or task
  • Identify any tools or processes (computer programs, team efforts, equipment) used to accomplish the above
  • Use numbers to show results and scope/scale of your success
  • Be represented as a bullet point (not written as a complete sentence. No personal pronouns or periods)
  • Example: Accomplished [skill/task] as measured by [results] by doing [tools/processes]

Success Statement Examples

  • Engineered network simulator in a Linux environment using Java as a proof of concept for research on proposed satellite technology
  • Formulated and executed test plans and test cases for functional testing of software
  • Worked in a team of five interns to design, install, test, and debug new software system
  • Refined communication skills by providing violin instruction in both one-on-one and group settings of up to 10 students
  • Learned to multi-taks, problem-solve, and pay attention to detail
  • Developed and improved ability to relate well with all types of people
  • Refined interpersonal communication skills while conduncting telephone surveys daily
  • Chaired the Student Affairs committee with four officers and three general members
  • Learned to effectively manage time and balance multiple projects in a fast-paced environment

Remember...

  • Resumes get less than a one-minute scan
  • Write a targeted resume for the company you are applying for
  • Be sure your degree and key skills/experiences are at the beginning of your resume
  • Review/research job descriptions on the company's website; focus on key words and skills
  • One page per 10 years of experience(consider two pages only if you are a returning or graduate student)
  • No grammar, spelling, typos, or format errors
  • Avoid templates and large blocks of text
  • Include a well-written, targeted cover letter
